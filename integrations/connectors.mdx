Connectors let Bird read from the tools where your lab's work already lives. Ask a question and Bird can search across your connected Drive, Notion, Slack, GitHub, and others — alongside the notes already in Bower — and ground its answer with citations from each.

Items only land in your knowledge base when you explicitly ask Bird to save them. Federated search by default; ingestion only on request.

## What Bird already searches — without any setup

Before you connect anything, Bird already grounds answers in several sources that are always on:

- **Your Bower workspace** — every note, protocol, collection, and attachment you've captured. Searched with semantic relevance and full citations.
- **The open scientific record** via two integrations that need no login:
  - **ORCID** — public publication histories. Bird can list a researcher's works by their ORCID iD.
  - **OpenAlex** — the open catalogue of ~250M scholarly works. Bird searches it for related literature and fetches full records by DOI.
- **The live web** — when a question reaches beyond your workspace and the open scientific record, Bird searches the web (including indexed scholarly sources like Google Scholar, PubMed Central, and journal sites). Results are returned with links so you can verify.

These four sources cover most research questions on their own. **Connectors below extend Bird into the tools you've licensed or own** — your Drive, your Notion, your team's Slack — where the rest of your work already lives.

## Per-user, per-workspace by design

Connectors are **personal**. Every member of a workspace connects their own tools, and Bird searches each user's connections separately when answering their questions. Your colleague's Drive isn't visible from your account, and your home workspace's Slack isn't pulled into your work workspace.

This is the safest model for two reasons:

- **OAuth tokens are bound to the person who authorised them.** When Bird searches your Drive on your behalf, it sees only what your Google account has access to. The provider does the access control — Bower can't accidentally surface a file you weren't allowed to see.
- **Different identities per workspace.** If you have separate "Personal" and "Work" workspaces, you can connect a personal Google account to one and your work Google account to the other. They stay cleanly separated.

When a new colleague joins the workspace, they'll see an empty Connectors tab and connect their own tools. There's no shared workspace-level connection at the moment — that's a feature on our roadmap, not a current capability.

## How to connect a tool

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Account Settings → Connectors">
    Click your avatar in the top right, choose **Account Settings**, then open the **Connectors** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Connect on the tool you want">
    Each tool has a **Connect** button on its card. Connecting one tool doesn't expose any others — Bird only searches what you've explicitly enabled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorise in the provider's window">
    You'll be sent to the provider's normal sign-in screen — Google, Microsoft, GitHub, and so on. Sign in with the account whose data you want Bird to access, review the permissions, and approve.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You're back in Bower">
    The card flips to **Connected** when authorisation completes. If something goes wrong on the provider's side, you'll see a toast explaining what happened — try again from the same card.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How your data and credentials are handled

We've built connectors so the sensitive parts stay where they should: at the provider, at the gateway, and out of Bower's database.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Bower never sees your password">
    Authorisation runs through the provider's standard OAuth flow. You sign in with the provider directly; Bower never touches your credentials.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="OAuth tokens never live in Bower">
    The access token your provider issues is held by our managed gateway (Composio) — not by Bower. Bower stores only an opaque connection identifier so we know which token to ask the gateway to use when you run a query.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Searches respect provider permissions">
    Bird sees only what your account has access to in the provider — same files, same channels, same repositories. If your admin removes your access to a Drive folder, Bird loses access to it the next time you search.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Read-only by default">
    Connectors are configured for read-only federated search. Bird doesn't write back to your connected tools. The only path for connector data to enter your knowledge base is when you explicitly ask Bird to save a specific result — for example, *"save this Google Doc as a note."* Until then, Bird just reads.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Saved items keep their provenance">
    Anything you save from a connected tool records the source URL and which connection it came from. You can always trace a saved note back to its origin.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Disconnecting actually disconnects">
    When you click Disconnect, the connection is revoked at the gateway and the token is destroyed. Anything previously saved into your knowledge base stays put — disconnecting only stops *future* searches.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="HIPAA workspaces can't use connectors yet">
    Our gateway provider's BAA isn't signed, so connectors are blocked in PHI-enabled workspaces. This is enforced server-side: even if the UI showed the option, the API would refuse the connection. We'll enable connectors in PHI workspaces once the BAA is in place.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Picking which connectors are active in a chat

Connecting a tool makes it *available* to Bird. To actually use it in a conversation, open the **connector picker** in the composer (next to the chat input) and tick which connectors should be active for that chat.

This is more than a convenience — it's a precision control. The more sources Bird can search, the slower and less focused it gets. Scoping a chat to "just Slack and GitHub" makes Bird's answer faster and more accurate when that's what you actually want it to look at.

The picker remembers your selection per chat, so you can keep separate threads scoped to different sources.

## Tier limits

Connectors are capped per user, per workspace.

| Plan | Connected tools |
| --- | --- |
| Starter | 2 |
| Pro | 10 |
| Team | Unlimited |

If you hit the cap, you'll see an upgrade prompt on the **Connect** button. The cap is on the number of *connected* tools, not the number you can browse. Disconnecting one frees up a slot.

## Reconnecting and disconnecting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Re-auth — when a tool stops working">
    OAuth tokens expire. When that happens the card shows a **Re-auth** badge and Bird stops using that connector until you reconnect. Click **Reconnect**; the provider walks you through a short re-authorisation, usually without re-entering your password.

    If you'd rather walk away from the connection entirely, the **Disconnect** button on the same card revokes it without re-authorising.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Disconnecting a tool">
    Open the connector card and click **Disconnect**. The connection is revoked at the gateway and the card returns to its unconnected state. Anything you previously saved into your knowledge base from that tool stays put — disconnecting only stops *future* searches.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Switching the underlying account">
    To switch from one Drive account to another (for example), disconnect the existing one and click Connect again. The next OAuth flow lets you sign in with the new account.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Cancelling a stuck connection attempt">
    If an OAuth dance was interrupted (you closed the browser tab, the provider timed out, you cancelled), the card stays in a **Connecting…** state and counts toward your cap. Click **Disconnect** on the card to discard the stuck attempt and free the slot.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What if I don't see a tool I expect?

A few things to check:

- **Is it listed above?** We curate the connector list to tools researchers actually use. If it isn't there, it isn't supported yet.
- **Did you reach the tier cap?** Connected tools count against your plan's cap; disconnect an unused one and try again.
- **Are you in a HIPAA workspace?** Connectors are disabled in PHI-enabled workspaces until our gateway provider's BAA is signed.
- **Did your colleague connect it instead?** Connectors are per-user — your teammate's connections aren't visible from your account. Open the tool's card and click Connect to set up your own.

For anything else, [reach out](/workspaces-and-account/feedback) and tell us what you were trying to do.
