Connectors let Bird read from the tools where your lab's work already lives. Ask a question and Bird can search across your connected Drive, Notion, Slack, GitHub, and others — alongside the notes already in Bower — and ground its answer with citations from each.

Items only land in your knowledge base when you explicitly ask Bird to save them. Federated search by default; ingestion only on request.

## What you can connect

You can connect any of the tools below. Each one is connected per user, per workspace — your colleague's Drive isn't visible from your account, and your home workspace's Slack isn't pulled into your work workspace.

    - **Google Sheets** — read spreadsheets

## How to connect a tool

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Account Settings → Connectors">
    Click your avatar in the top right, choose **Account Settings**, then open the **Connectors** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Connect on the tool you want">
    Each tool has a **Connect** button on its card. Bird needs to be told which tools to use, so connecting one doesn't expose any others.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorise in the provider's window">
    You'll be sent to the provider's normal sign-in screen — Google, Microsoft, GitHub, and so on. Sign in, review the permissions, and approve.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You're back in Bower">
    The card flips to **Connected** when authorisation completes. If something goes wrong on the provider's side, you'll see a toast explaining what happened — try again from the same card.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Bower never sees your password. Authorisation runs through the provider's standard OAuth flow, and the access token is held by our managed gateway, not stored in Bower.
</Note>

## Picking which connectors are active in a chat

Connecting a tool makes it *available* to Bird. To actually use it in a conversation, open the **connector picker** in the composer (next to the chat input) and tick which connectors should be active for that chat.

This means you can have, say, ten tools connected but only ask Bird to search Slack and GitHub for the question you're working on. It also keeps Bird focused — the more sources it has to consider, the slower and less precise it gets.

The picker remembers your selection per chat, so you can keep separate threads scoped to different sources.

## What Bird does with your data

Connectors are **read-only by default**. Bird searches your connected tools to ground answers; it doesn't write back to them. The only way data from a connected tool ends up in your knowledge base is when **you** ask Bird to save a specific result — for example, "save this Google Doc as a note." Until then, Bird just reads.

A few specifics worth knowing:

- **Tokens never live in Bower.** Our managed gateway (Composio) holds the OAuth tokens; Bower stores only an opaque connection identifier.
- **Searches respect provider permissions.** Bird sees only what your account has access to in the provider — same files, same channels, same repos.
- **Saved items keep their provenance.** Anything you save from a connected tool records the source URL and the connection it came from, so you can always trace back.
- **HIPAA workspaces** can't use connectors yet. Our gateway provider's BAA isn't signed, so connectors are blocked in PHI-enabled workspaces. We'll enable them once that lands.

## Tier limits

Connectors are capped per user, per workspace.

| Plan | Connected tools |
| --- | --- |
| Starter | 2 |
| Pro | 10 |
| Team | Unlimited |

If you hit the cap, you'll see an upgrade prompt on the **Connect** button. The cap is on the number of *connected* tools, not the number you can browse.

## Reconnecting and disconnecting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Re-auth — when a tool stops working">
    OAuth tokens expire. When that happens the card shows a **Re-auth** badge and Bird stops using that connector until you reconnect. Click **Connect** again on the card; the provider walks you through a short re-authorisation, usually without re-entering your password.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Disconnecting a tool">
    Open the connector card and click **Disconnect**. The connection is revoked at the gateway and the card returns to its unconnected state. Anything you previously saved into your knowledge base from that tool stays put — disconnecting only stops *future* searches.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Switching the underlying account">
    To switch from one Drive account to another (for example), disconnect the existing one and click Connect again. The next OAuth flow lets you sign in with the new account.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What if I don't see a tool I expect?

A few things to check:

- **Is it listed above?** We curate the connector list to tools researchers actually use. If it isn't there, it isn't supported yet.
- **Did you reach the tier cap?** Connected tools count against your plan's cap; uninstall an unused one and try again.
- **Are you in a HIPAA workspace?** Connectors are disabled in PHI-enabled workspaces until our gateway provider's BAA is signed.

For anything else, [reach out](/workspaces-and-account/feedback) and tell us what you were trying to do.
