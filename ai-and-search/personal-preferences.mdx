title: "Personal preferences and memory"
description: "Help Bird understand how you work with personal preferences and private learned memories."
---

Bird can personalise its help in three different ways. They have different
authors, audiences, and purposes.

| Type | Who creates it | Who can see it | Use it for |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Personal preferences** | You | Only you | How you like Bird to help: your role, expertise, writing style, and recurring preferences |
| **What Bird remembers** | Bird, after processing a conversation turn and applying a memory change | Only you | Useful, durable details Bird has learned from working with you |
| [**Workspace instructions**](/ai-and-search/workspace-instructions) | Workspace owners and admins | Everyone in the workspace | Shared lab conventions, vocabulary, and standing guidance |

All three are available on every Bower plan.

## Personal preferences

**Personal preferences** are a note you write for Bird. Use them for details
you want to state directly, such as the depth of explanation you prefer, your
field, or how you want results formatted.

For example:

```
I'm a postdoc in computational neuroscience. Default to concise answers,
include SI units, and use bullet points for summaries.
```

Set or update them in **Settings → Preferences → Personal preferences for
Bird**. Keep them focused on preferences that are likely to remain useful.

## What Bird remembers

**What Bird remembers** is a list of explicit, learned memories. Bird may save
a useful, durable detail after it processes a conversation turn and applies a
memory change. This can include a fact or preference Bird learns during a
normal chat.

Bird confirms this in the chat only after it saves a memory.

Memory capture happens in the background, so it should not slow Bird's
response to you.

### Review or remove learned memories

Learned memories are private to you. Other members of your workspace,
including owners and admins, cannot see or edit them.

Open **Settings → Preferences → What Bird remembers** to view, edit, or delete
any learned memory. Remove anything that is no longer accurate or helpful.

## Workspace instructions

Use [workspace instructions](/ai-and-search/workspace-instructions) for the
guidance a whole lab or organisation should share with Bird: terminology,
standing conventions, and team-wide expectations. They are not a place for
your personal preferences or private learned memories.

<Tip>
  Put a preference in the layer that owns it: write your own preferences,
  review Bird's learned memories, and keep team guidance in workspace
  instructions.
</Tip>
