You can now connect Claude.ai, ChatGPT, Cursor, and Zed to your Bower workspace
so your AI assistant can search, read, and write directly to your notes,
projects, and experiment data. Wherever you are working, Bird's workspace
knowledge is available without switching context.

**Shipped 2026-05-31 · Integrations**

## What's new

- **Claude.ai** — add Bower as an MCP connector; native refresh-token support
  keeps the session alive.
- **ChatGPT** — add Bower as a custom MCP connector (Pro plan or higher).
- **Cursor** — configure Bower via `~/.cursor/mcp.json` or Settings → MCP / Tools.
- **Zed** — configure Bower via `~/.config/zed/settings.json` using the
  `mcp-remote` shim (verify native HTTP support in your version).
- A new **AI app access** tab in Settings shows which clients have connected and
  lets you manage access.

All four clients connect to the same endpoint: `https://api.bowerlabs.ai/mcp`.

## How it works

Connection uses OAuth 2.1 + PKCE. When you add Bower as an MCP server in your
client, the client opens a browser tab to Bower's sign-in page. Sign in with
your Bower account — Google or email — and, if you belong to more than one
workspace, pick which one to grant the client access to. Bower issues scoped
tokens and redirects you back to the client. The whole flow takes under a minute.

Tokens are issued and rotated by WorkOS AuthKit, Bower's identity partner.
Refresh tokens rotate automatically, so long-running client sessions stay
connected without you having to re-authorise. Bower stores no credentials of its
own — all token lifecycle management runs through WorkOS.

The scopes issued at connect time are `mcp:read` (read workspace data) and
`mcp:write` (create and modify data on your behalf). You authorise both when you
connect. Workspace isolation is server-enforced on every request — a token
scoped to workspace A never returns data from workspace B, even if you are a
member of both.

## Privacy

- Bower never sees your AI client's credentials. Only OAuth tokens are exchanged.
- Tokens are issued and rotated by WorkOS AuthKit. Bower stores no access tokens.
- Workspace isolation is server-enforced — valid tokens for one workspace never
  surface data from another.

## Tier limits

AI app access is available on all paid Bower tiers. HIPAA-enabled workspaces are
not yet supported — our identity provider's BAA is not yet signed. Server-side
enforced until that is in place.
